Our Story

DeLand After Dark

When the night goes quiet in Volusia County, Radio Free DeLand is still on the air — and someone is always there to pick up.

There's a particular stillness that settles over DeLand around 2 a.m. The orange groves go dark, the courthouse square empties out, and the Spanish moss stops swaying. But somewhere on the dial, a light stays on. Radio Free DeLand has been that light — a 24-hour call-in broadcast rooted in the rhythms of this community, running through every humid Florida night without pause.

It started simply enough: a belief that local voices deserve a live platform at any hour. Not a playlist. Not a bot. A real person, a live mic, and an open phone line. Whether you're a night-shift nurse heading home on US-17, a college student burning the midnight oil, or a retiree who just can't sleep — when you dial in, someone answers.

"Radio Free DeLand is a live front porch for this community — the kind of place where you can pull up a chair at any hour and find a familiar voice waiting."

— The Spirit of the Station

The callers are what make it real. Familiar voices that have been checking in for years. First-timers who stumble onto the frequency and can't believe someone picks up. Local musicians calling to share a new track. Neighbors talking through a storm warning. Listeners from across Florida — and beyond — who found something in this small-city broadcast that the big markets never quite offered: genuine, unscripted community radio.

This is broadcasting as it was always meant to feel — immediate, human, and alive. Tune in tonight. The porch light is on.
